# Demo Script

## Minute-by-minute walkthrough
**Total runtime: 2 minutes 45 seconds**
*Rehearse this at least twice. Every transition is a spoken cue.*

---

### 0:00 – 0:30 — Hook (spoken, no demo yet)

> "You're in a client meeting. They've just asked what the chalet looks like if you widen the living area by two meters. You know that means a CAD redraw — 30 to 90 minutes of work, another email, another meeting. That's the gap we closed."

*[Open the browser tab. The 3D chalet is already loaded. Do not explain the screen yet — let it land.]*

---

### 0:30 – 1:00 — First impression: the model speaks for itself

*[Slowly orbit the model using the mouse — one full rotation.]*

> "This runs in a browser tab. No install, no server, no GPU. It loaded in under two seconds."

*[Point to the dimension labels visible on the geometry.]*

> "These annotations are anchored directly to the 3D geometry — not a legend, not a table. The client sees the number on the wall."

---

### 1:00 – 1:40 — Live dimension change (the core moment)

> "Now watch what happens when the client says: wider, and lower the roof."

*[Move the Width slider from its default to a larger value — pause 1 second after releasing.]*

> "Under 100 milliseconds. The geometry rebuilds. The annotations update. Nothing to re-export."

*[Move the Roof Pitch slider downward — pause again.]*

> "The client can say yes or no on the spot. That's the shift from 'trust the drawing' to 'see it live.'"

*[Orbit the model again to show the updated geometry from a different angle.]*

---

### 1:40 – 2:15 — Presets: showing range of use cases

> "We also built three presets — Small Chalet, Large Chalet, and Studio — to show this isn't a one-off demo."

*[Click the Large Chalet preset. Let the model update.]*

> "One click. The full model switches. Different dimensions, same live behavior."

*[Click the Studio preset.]*

> "Any of these can be handed to a client as a starting point, and they can adjust from there."

---

### 2:15 – 2:45 — Close: impact and next step

> "What you're looking at is a single HTML file. No framework, no build step, no backend. Any Bentley engineer can open the source in minutes — and the natural next step is connecting it to real iTwin project data."

> "The prototype already hits every target we set: load in under two seconds, dimension update in under 100 milliseconds, runs on any standard laptop. The question we answered today is: can we collapse the client sign-off loop from days to minutes? The answer is yes — and you just watched it happen."

*[Leave the model rotating on screen as Q&A begins.]*

---

## Presenter notes

- **Never apologize for the simplicity.** The simplicity is the point — zero install is a feature, not a limitation.
- **Let pauses breathe.** After each slider move, wait a full second before speaking. The visual update does the work.
- **Orbit slowly.** Fast orbiting is disorienting for non-technical viewers. One rotation in about 4 seconds is enough.
- **If a slider misbehaves:** Say "the geometry rebuilds on every change" and move a different slider. Do not stop.
- **If asked about mobile:** "It runs on any device with a browser — mobile optimization is a next-iteration item." Move on.
