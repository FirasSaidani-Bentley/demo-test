# Problem

## What we solve

Architects, contractors, and clients lose time communicating building dimensions. Today, a dimension change in a 2D plan requires manually recalculating, redrawing, and re-explaining the impact. There is no way to instantly visualize how changing the width, height, or roof pitch of a chalet affects the full structure in real time. Errors caught late cost money. Clients struggle to read flat plans.

## Who we solve it for

Architects and construction professionals presenting chalet or timber-frame building designs to clients. Also non-technical clients who need to understand and validate dimensions before sign-off. Target users have no 3D modeling expertise and cannot afford to wait for a full CAD render every time a dimension changes.

## Why now

Construction visualization tools are either too complex (full BIM suites like MicroStation or Revit) or too static (PDF exports). Browser-based 3D rendering with Three.js is now mature enough to run without installation. Clients expect interactive experiences. A prototype that runs in a browser tab, with no download, changes the conversation from "trust the plan" to "see it live".
