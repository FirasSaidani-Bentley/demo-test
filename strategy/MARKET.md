# Market

## Target users

Architects and project engineers using Bentley tools (MicroStation, OpenBuildings) who present designs to non-technical clients. Also end clients in the residential and light commercial construction market who need to validate building dimensions before sign-off. Bentley serves over 5,000 infrastructure and construction firms globally.

## Competition

- **Revit / ArchiCAD**: Full BIM suites. Require hours of setup per model, trained operators, and powerful hardware. Not usable by a client during a meeting.
- **SketchUp Web**: General-purpose 3D, no parametric dimension control, no live annotations.
- **PDF exports from MicroStation**: Static, 2D, require re-export for every change.
- **Custom Three.js demos**: Exist as one-off prototypes, never productized or connected to real project data.

None of these let a non-technical user change a slider and immediately see the annotated 3D result.

## Context

Browser-based 3D (Three.js, WebGL) is now production-grade and runs on a laptop without GPU requirements. The construction industry is under pressure to reduce rework costs, which average 5-10% of project value. Clients increasingly expect digital twins and interactive models. Bentley's strategic direction toward iTwin and connected data makes a lightweight parametric viewer a natural entry point.
